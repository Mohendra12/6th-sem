Q.1 what is magnification?

Ans: Magnification is the process of enlarging the appearance of an object using an optical instrument, such as a microscope or lens, and is calculated as the ratio of the size of the image to the size of the object.

Q.2 What is fluorescence microscopy?

Fluorescence microscopy is a technique that uses fluorescent dyes or proteins to label specific structures or molecules in a sample. When exposed to specific wavelengths of light, these fluorescent substances emit light at a longer wavelength, allowing visualization of the targeted components with high specificity and contrast.

Q.3 What is whole chromosome painting?

Whole Chromosome Painting (WCP) is a cytogenetic technique that uses fluorescently labeled DNA probes specific to an entire chromosome. These probes hybridize to their corresponding chromosomes during fluorescence in situ hybridization (FISH) , allowing visualization of the entire chromosome in different colors for detecting structural abnormalities or identifying chromosomal origins.
